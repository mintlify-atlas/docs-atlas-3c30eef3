## Overview

The Advanced Track (Weeks 9-16) takes you from foundational transformer knowledge to research-grade capabilities in distributed systems, safety alignment, interpretability, and specialized domains including computer vision, reinforcement learning, and multimodal systems.

<Info>
  **Time Commitment**: 8 weeks at 10-15 hours per week
  
  **Prerequisites**: Completion of Foundations Track (Weeks 1-8) or equivalent knowledge
</Info>

## Learning Arc

The advanced curriculum follows a strategic progression:

1. **Systems Scaling** (Week 9): Distributed training and efficient inference
2. **Evaluation Rigor** (Week 10): Benchmarks, metrics, and statistical validation
3. **Safety Alignment** (Week 11): Reward modeling and policy regularization
4. **Interpretability** (Week 12): Mechanistic understanding of model internals
5. **Specialized Domains** (Weeks 13-15): Vision, RL, and multimodal systems
6. **Research Capstone** (Week 16): Experiment design and paper reproduction

## Core Advanced Topics (Weeks 9-12)

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Week 9: Distributed Training and Inference">
    **Core Formula**: `g_avg = (1 / W) * sum(g_worker)`
    
    Simulate gradient synchronization and understand why averaging keeps updates stable.
    
    **Learning Outcomes**:
    - Implement data parallelism with gradient averaging
    - Understand model parallelism and pipeline parallelism
    - Design efficient inference systems with batching and caching
    
    **Coding Challenge**: Average Worker Gradients (30-45 min)
    
    **Key References**:
    - JAX Scaling Book
    - Distributed training best practices from DeepSpeed and Megatron
    
    **Why This Matters**: Modern LLMs require distributed training across hundreds of GPUs. Understanding synchronization primitives and parallelism strategies is essential for research-scale work.

      Master distributed systems
    </Card>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Week 10: Evaluation and Benchmarks">
    **Focus**: Metrics, benchmarks, statistical significance, and leaderboard interpretation
    
    Learn to rigorously evaluate model capabilities and understand what benchmarks actually measure.
    
    **Learning Outcomes**:
    - Implement perplexity, BLEU, ROUGE, and task-specific metrics
    - Understand benchmark construction and potential biases
    - Conduct statistical significance testing for model comparisons
    
    **Coding Challenge**: Benchmark evaluation pipeline (40-60 min)
    
    **Key Topics**:
    - Language modeling metrics (perplexity, bits-per-character)
    - Generation quality (BLEU, ROUGE, BERTScore)
    - Reasoning benchmarks (MMLU, GSM8K, HumanEval)
    - Contamination detection and dataset leakage
    
    <Card title="Start Week 10" href="/weeks/week10-evaluation-benchmarks">
      Learn rigorous evaluation
    </Card>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Week 11: Alignment and Safety Objectives">
    **Core Formula**: `J = E_pi[r] - beta * KL(pi || pi_ref)`
    
    Study how reward optimization is balanced by policy regularization terms.
    
    **Learning Outcomes**:
    - Understand RLHF (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback)
    - Implement KL-regularized reward objectives
    - Explore constitutional AI and value alignment approaches
    
    **Coding Challenge**: KL-Regularized Objective (40-60 min)
    
    **Key References**:
    - Anthropic Research on constitutional AI
    - OpenAI Research on RLHF and InstructGPT
    - Lilian Weng's blog on alignment techniques
    
    **Safety Considerations**:
    - Reward hacking and specification gaming
    - Value alignment and intent inference
    - Robustness to adversarial inputs
    
    <Card title="Start Week 11" href="/weeks/week11-alignment-safety">
      Explore alignment and safety
    </Card>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Week 12: Interpretability and Mechanistic Understanding">
    **Focus**: Attention visualization, activation analysis, and circuit discovery
    
    Open the black box: understand what transformer models are actually computing.
    
    **Learning Outcomes**:
    - Visualize and interpret attention patterns
    - Conduct activation patching and causal interventions
    - Identify interpretable features and circuits
    
    **Coding Challenge**: Attention analysis and interpretation (45-60 min)
    
    **Key Techniques**:
    - Attention head analysis and pattern discovery
    - Logit lens and residual stream analysis
    - Mechanistic interpretability and circuit extraction
    - Feature visualization and neuron analysis
    
    **Research Frontier**: This week connects to cutting-edge work at Anthropic (dictionary learning), OpenAI (superposition), and independent researchers (mechanistic interpretability).

## Specialized Domain Tracks (Weeks 13-15)

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Computer Vision (Week 13)">
    **Core Formula**: `patch_count = (height / patch_size) * (width / patch_size)`
    
    ### Vision Transformers
    
    Patchify images and reason about how ViT-style tokenization works.
    
    **Learning Outcomes**:
    - Implement image patchification and positional embeddings
    - Understand Vision Transformer (ViT) architecture
    - Compare convolutional vs. attention-based vision models
    
    **Coding Challenge**: Image Patchifier (40-60 min)
    
    **Key Topics**:
    - Patch embeddings and CLS tokens
    - Hybrid architectures (ConvNets + Transformers)
    - Object detection with DETR
    - Segmentation with Mask2Former
    
    **Key References**:
    - Meta AI Blog on vision research
    - "An Image is Worth 16x16 Words" (ViT paper)
    - DETR and Swin Transformer papers
    
    <Card title="Start Week 13" href="/weeks/week13-computer-vision-transformers">
      Explore vision transformers
    </Card>
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Reinforcement Learning (Week 14)">
    ### RL with Transformers
    
    Apply transformer architectures to sequential decision-making and reinforcement learning.
    
    **Learning Outcomes**:
    - Implement Decision Transformers
    - Understand offline RL and trajectory modeling
    - Connect language modeling to policy learning
    
    **Coding Challenge**: Transformer-based RL agent (45-60 min)
    
    **Key Topics**:
    - Decision Transformers and Trajectory Transformers
    - Return-conditioned policies
    - Multi-task RL with transformer backbones
    - Prompt-based control and in-context learning for RL
    
    **Why This Matters**: The connection between language modeling and RL is fundamental to understanding systems like ChatGPT (RLHF), code generation agents, and autonomous decision-making systems.
    
    <Card title="Start Week 14" href="/weeks/week14-reinforcement-learning-transformers">
      Apply transformers to RL
    </Card>
  </Tab>

    **Coding Challenge**: Multimodal alignment (45-60 min)
    
    **Key Topics**:
    - Contrastive pre-training (CLIP, ALIGN)
    - Vision-language generation (Flamingo, BLIP)
    - Audio-visual learning
    - Unified multimodal architectures
    
    **Research Applications**:
    - Image captioning and VQA
    - Text-to-image generation guidance

## Research Capstone (Week 16)

<Card title="Week 16: Research Capstone and Experiment Design">
  **Core Formula**: `CI = [percentile(alpha/2), percentile(1 - alpha/2)]`
  
  ### Culminating Project
  
  Summarize experiment variability with bootstrap confidence intervals and conduct rigorous research.
  
  **Learning Outcomes**:
  - Design and execute research experiments
  - Reproduce results from recent AI papers
  - Compute confidence intervals and statistical significance
  - Document findings with academic rigor
  
  **Coding Challenge**: Bootstrap Confidence Interval (45-60 min)
  
  **Capstone Activities**:
  1. Choose a recent AI paper (from OpenAI, Anthropic, DeepMind, etc.)
  2. Reproduce core experimental results
  3. Conduct ablation studies
  4. Validate findings with statistical tests
  5. Document methodology and insights
  
  **Key Skills**:
  - Experimental design and hypothesis testing
  - Reproducibility and open science practices
  - Technical writing and documentation
  - Version control and experiment tracking
